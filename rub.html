About Me


Growing up, I used to doodle on the walls of my room - trees, sticks, people,flowers. The same was conquered in countless pages of my notebooks.

So when my school introduced a course called “Computing and Designing”, I eagerly signed up for it, thinking it was a graphic designing class. Imagine my disappointment when the teacher told me the closest I would come to designing would be creating a circle in QBASIC.

To my surprise, I really enjoyed that class. As coding required fresh thinking, imagination and the willingness to experiment, it felt like an art class that required problem solving. That's how I was first introduced to computer science

Programming and painting continue to be an integral part of my life. At Swarthmore Collge, I am pursuing a double major in Computer Science and Studio Arts.

My art portfolio mainly consits of oil and acrylic paintings on canvas.


I am also and active member of Women in Computer Science at Swarthmore College, where along with gaining exposure to tech, I am also fostering a supportive environment for women passionate about Computer Science.


Interests

I love building things. While challenging engineering and math problems are fun to tackle, I'm most interested in binding my computer science knowledge with social justice, so that I can create products that can connect human needs to help organizations and people.

As a Lang Oppurtunity Scholar, I created a web platform that connects at-risk women with their potential employers for expediting employment creation.

Through my Lang Project, in partnership with Himlayan Climate Initiative, we also also ran different trainings for the young and less-educated women in order to provide them livelihood opportunities within Nepal.

I'm looking for oppurtunities where I can work with collaborative teams to gain experience in writing well documented and efficient code. I am also interested in project management and development roles.
